2. Shore Based Personnel - HRM

2.1 Recruitment and selection
Note:FFM maintains a preference to recruit within before looking outside the organisation. In case of any vacancy, internal applicants must inform their Line Manger/ Head of Department prior to officially applying. The Line Manager informs the HRS Manager.
  1. The Head of HR decides the method of the recruitment
  2. The Head of HR screens the CV
  3. The Head of HR forwards the suitable CV to the Line Manager/ Head of Department 
  4. The Head of HR and Line Manager/ Head of Department :
    • Agree on shortlisted candidates
    • Schedule interviews
Note:All candidates that are shortlisted for second interview must complete a psychometric test
Caution: Head of HR or Line Manager/ Head of Department cannot conduct the interview if they are relatives or close friend of the candidate. The HR Department provides alternative interviewers.
  • Conduct interviews
  • Agree on a suitable candidate
  1. The Head of HR:
    • Makes the employment offer
    • Verifies the authenticity of the qualifications and reference letters provided by the candidate
    • Ensures the employeeâs right to work in the country of employment
    • Ensures that the employee is medically fit to work in the position offered
    • Circulates the employee pre-joining form to all parties concerned
  1. Head of HR:
    • Informs the Managing Director (MD) of the selected employee(s) or team

    • Informs winning employee(s) via email

    • Announces the winning employee(s)

  1. Marine and technical competencies. It is part of our HR policy to have at least:
    • 1 Marine Superintendent with a valid Master license
    • 1 Technical Superintendent with a valid Chief Engineers license

Consequently, we are guided by the same in our recruitment of superintendents and in the support we give to existing superintendents to maintain their CoC.

Note:To support vessels during emergency FFM maintains an overview of available Masters and Chief Engineers who might assist the vessel urgently.

2.2 Learning and development
Note:Training includes coaching, mentoring, shadowing, internal and external training, self-study, e-learning.
  1. The Head of HR:
    • Prepares the shore staff training matrix
    • Sources the training providers
    • Confirms staff availability
    • Schedules the training
    • Reviews the effectiveness of the process
  1. The Employee:
    • Attends the training
    • Completes the training evaluation form after the training
    • Submits the form to the Head of HR
  1. The Line Manager evaluates the benefit of the training during the end-year appraisal
  2. All certificates and training records are to be uploaded to the Simployer
Caution: Line Managers ensure proper office attendance to cover all operational aspects.
Note:To support vessels during emergency FFM maintains an overview of available Masters and Chief Engineers who might assist the vessel urgently. In addition, it is part of our HR policy to have at least 1 valid Master license and 1 valid Chief Engineers license among our Marine and Technical Superintendents. Consequently, we are guided by the same in our recruitment of superintendents and in the support we give for existing superintendents to maintain their CoC.

2.6 Handing over responsibilities
  1. Determine the scope and depth of the handover based on:
    • Responsibilities of the persons involved
    • Experience of the person taking over
    • Duration of the handover
      • Permanent
      • Temporary
  1. Department Head ensures:
    • Adequate manning of the department
    • Back-up arrangements in place
    • Handing over of responsibilities to a dedicated back-up if the employee will be absent for leave or business travel
    • Communication of proper and detailed Handover notes to all concerned employees
Note:

Handover notes are required for an absence of 3 days or more, use FFM Shore Handover notes form.

Discuss handover notes with the assigned back-up and the immediate superior.

  • Briefings of returning staff by the dedicated back up about the status of responsibilities

2.7 Expenses
  1. The employee completes the Personal Expense form:
    • For cash advances within 15 working days after return from a trip
    • For credit cards within 30 working days after receipt of the bank statement
Note:If the employee is still travelling within the above timeframes, they must submit the claim form within two weeks of their return to the office.
  1. The employee attaches original receipts for all items on the form
  2. The employeeâs Line Manager approves the form
  3. The employee submits the form to the Finance department for reimbursement
Note:Personal credit cards can be used for business related expenses. The above procedure will apply for reimbursement.

2.17 Informal grievance - stage 1
Note:
Try to resolve any grievance directly.
  1. Request a meeting with your Line Manager
  2. Discuss your grievance
  3. Provide any supporting evidence
  4. Allow your Line Manager time to resolve the grievance
Note:Follow formal grievance procedure if issue is not resolved.

2. Shore Based Personnel - HRM

2.18 Formal grievance - stage 2
Note:Proceed to this stage if the informal approach does not resolve the grievance.
  1. Submit Grievance form HRS 010 to the Head of HR
  2. Head of HR then has 10 working days to review
  3. Agree the date of grievance hearing
  4. Head of HR provides any documentation to all parties prior to the meeting
  5. Attend the grievance hearing
  6. Receive decision from the Head of HR in writing
Note:Follow appeal procedure if issue is not resolved.

2. Shore Based Personnel - HRM

2.19 Disciplinary
Note:Try to resolve any minor infractions without utilising the formal disciplinary procedure.

Head of HR fills out Form HRS 011 

Note:If there is a genuine disciplinary case the following steps apply:
  1. Head of HR informs the employee of the problem in writing
  2. Head of HR invites the participants to the disciplinary meeting
  3. Hold the meeting with the employee and companion to discuss the problem
  4. Evaluate the evidence after the meeting
  5. Decide on appropriate action
  6. Head of HR informs the employee of the decision in writing
Note:Follow appeal procedure if issue is not resolved.

2. Shore Based Personnel - HRM

2.20 Disciplinary appeal
Note:Proceed to this stage if you are unhappy with the formal disciplinary decision.
  1. Submit the Disciplinary form within 5 working days of receiving the formal disciplinary decision
  2. Give the Head of HR time to review
  3. Agree the date of appeal hearing
  4. Attend the appeal hearing
  5. The Head of HR provides the completed Disciplinary Appeal form to the employee
